# Church Nonprofit Financial System Plan

Practical implementation plan for a nonprofit financial system for a church with two co-pastors.

## I. Core Requirements

- **Fund Accounting:** Track finances by designated funds (e.g., General Fund, Building Fund, Missions Fund).
- **Budgeting:** Create and manage annual budgets for each fund.
- **Donation Tracking:** Record and acknowledge all donations accurately, including donor information and fund allocation.
- **Expense Management:** Process and track all church expenses, ensuring proper authorization and documentation.
- **Reporting:** Generate regular financial reports for leadership and congregational transparency.
- **User Access Control:** Differentiate access levels for pastors, finance committee, and administrative staff.
- **Audit Trail:** Maintain a clear record of all financial transactions for accountability.

## II. Role/Approval Workflow

1. **Initiation:** Any authorized staff or committee member can initiate an expense request or budget proposal.
2. **Co-Pastor Review/Approval:** Both co-pastors review and approve all significant expenses (e.g., over a defined threshold, such as $500) and annual budgets. For smaller expenses, one co-pastor approval may suffice, with clear guidelines.
3. **Finance Committee Oversight:** The finance committee reviews financial reports, approves budgets, and provides recommendations to church leadership.
4. **Treasurer/Bookkeeper:** Handles daily transaction processing, bank reconciliations, and report generation.

## III. Chart of Accounts (Example)

### Assets

- Cash (General Fund Checking, Savings, Building Fund)
- Investments
- Property & Equipment

### Liabilities

- Accounts Payable
- Accrued Expenses
- Loans Payable

### Net Assets (Equity)

- Unrestricted Net Assets
- Temporarily Restricted Net Assets (for donor-designated funds)
- Permanently Restricted Net Assets (e.g., endowments)

### Revenue

- General Tithes & Offerings
- Designated Contributions (Building Fund, Missions)
- Program Fees (event registrations)
- Investment Income

### Expenses

- **Personnel:** Salaries, Benefits, Payroll Taxes
- **Occupancy:** Rent/Mortgage, Utilities, Maintenance
- **Program Services:** Ministry Supplies, Event Costs, Outreach
- **Administration:** Office Supplies, Insurance, Professional Fees
- **Stewardship/Development:** Donor Communication, Fundraising Costs

## IV. Donation and Expense Controls

### Donations

- Prompt, accurate donation receipts for tax purposes.
- Segregation of duties: person receiving donations should not be the same person recording them.
- Strict adherence to donor designations.

### Expenses

- Purchase orders/requisitions for expenses over a threshold.
- Mandatory receipts/invoices for reimbursements and payments.
- Budget vs. actual monitoring to prevent overspending.
- Monthly bank reconciliations for all accounts.

## V. Monthly Close/Reporting Cadence

- **Week 1:** Reconcile bank accounts, process outstanding invoices, finalize payroll.
- **Week 2:** Prepare/distribute preliminary financial statements (Statement of Financial Position and Statement of Activities) to co-pastors and finance committee.
- **Week 3:** Finance committee reviews reports, variances, and approves adjustments.
- **Week 4:** Finalize summary reports for congregational communication.

## VI. Compliance/Audit Considerations

- Keep accurate records for annual IRS filings (e.g., Form 990 where applicable).
- Comply with state charitable solicitation requirements.
- Implement and document robust internal controls.
- Consider annual audit/review by an independent CPA.

## VII. Recommended Tech Stack Options

### Accounting Software (cloud-based)

- **QuickBooks for Nonprofits:** Common and user-friendly.
- **Aplos:** Designed for churches/faith-based nonprofits.
- **Xero:** Modern UI, strong integrations.

### Donor Management / CRM

- Church management systems with integrated giving (e.g., Planning Center).
- Dedicated nonprofit CRMs (e.g., Bloomerang, Little Green Light).

### Document Storage

- Google Drive / Google Workspace
- Microsoft OneDrive / SharePoint

## VIII. 90-Day Rollout Plan

### Days 1–30: Planning & Setup

- Finalize requirements and chart of accounts.
- Select accounting platform.
- Configure chart of accounts, roles, permissions, and approval workflows.
- Plan data migration.

### Days 31–60: Data Migration & Training

- Migrate opening balances and key historical data.
- Train co-pastors, finance committee, and staff.
- Pilot a few weeks of transactions.
- Refine workflows/reports from pilot feedback.

### Days 61–90: Go-Live & Stabilization

- Move all operations to the new system.
- Complete first full month-end close/reporting cycle.
- Monitor adoption and system performance.
- Resolve remaining issues and schedule first finance committee review under the new process.
