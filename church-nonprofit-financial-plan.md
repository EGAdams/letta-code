# Church Nonprofit Financial System Plan (2 Co-Pastors)

Here's a practical implementation plan for a nonprofit financial system for a church with two co-pastors.

## I. Core Requirements

- **Fund Accounting:** Track finances by designated funds (e.g., General Fund, Building Fund, Missions Fund).
- **Budgeting:** Create and manage annual budgets for each fund.
- **Donation Tracking:** Record and acknowledge all donations accurately, including donor information and fund allocation.
- **Expense Management:** Process and track all church expenses, ensuring proper authorization and documentation.
- **Reporting:** Generate regular financial reports for leadership and congregational transparency.
- **User Access Control:** Differentiate access levels for pastors, finance committee, and administrative staff.
- **Audit Trail:** Maintain a clear record of all financial transactions for accountability.

## II. Role/Approval Workflow

1. **Initiation:** Any authorized staff or committee member can initiate an expense request or budget proposal.
2. **Co-Pastor Review/Approval:** Both co-pastors must review and approve all significant expenses (e.g., over a defined threshold, e.g., $500) and annual budgets. For smaller expenses, one co-pastor's approval may suffice, with clear guidelines.
3. **Finance Committee Oversight:** The finance committee reviews all financial reports, approves budgets, and provides recommendations to the church leadership.
4. **Treasurer/Bookkeeper:** Responsible for daily transaction processing, bank reconciliations, and generating reports.

## III. Chart of Accounts (Example — Adaptable)

- **Assets:**
  - Cash (e.g., General Fund Checking, Savings, Building Fund)
  - Investments
  - Property & Equipment
- **Liabilities:**
  - Accounts Payable
  - Accrued Expenses
  - Loans Payable
- **Net Assets (Equity):**
  - Unrestricted Net Assets
  - Temporarily Restricted Net Assets (e.g., for specific donor-designated funds)
  - Permanently Restricted Net Assets (rare for churches, e.g., endowments)
- **Revenue:**
  - General Tithes & Offerings
  - Designated Contributions (e.g., Building Fund, Missions)
  - Program Fees (e.g., event registrations)
  - Investment Income
- **Expenses:**
  - **Personnel:** Salaries, Benefits, Payroll Taxes
  - **Occupancy:** Rent/Mortgage, Utilities, Maintenance
  - **Program Services:** Ministry Supplies, Event Costs, Outreach
  - **Administration:** Office Supplies, Insurance, Professional Fees
  - **Stewardship/Development:** Donor Communication, Fundraising Costs

## IV. Donation and Expense Controls

- **Donations:**
  - **Receipting:** Prompt and accurate donation receipts for tax purposes.
  - **Segregation of Duties:** The person receiving donations should not be the same person recording them in the system.
  - **Designated Funds:** Strict adherence to donor designations.
- **Expenses:**
  - **Purchase Orders/Requisitions:** For expenses over a certain amount, requiring pre-approval.
  - **Receipts/Invoices:** Mandatory for all expense reimbursements and payments.
  - **Budget vs. Actual Tracking:** Regular monitoring to prevent overspending.
  - **Bank Reconciliations:** Monthly reconciliation of all bank accounts.

## V. Monthly Close/Reporting Cadence

- **Week 1:** Reconcile all bank accounts, process outstanding invoices, and finalize payroll.
- **Week 2:** Prepare and distribute preliminary financial statements (Statement of Financial Position, Statement of Activities) to co-pastors and finance committee.
- **Week 3:** Finance committee meeting to review reports, discuss variances, and approve any necessary adjustments.
- **Week 4:** Finalize reports for congregational distribution (e.g., summarized in a newsletter or presented at a meeting).

## VI. Compliance/Audit Considerations

- **IRS Form 990:** Ensure accurate record-keeping for annual IRS filings.
- **State Charitable Solicitation Registration:** Comply with any state-specific requirements.
- **Internal Controls:** Implement and document robust internal controls to prevent fraud and error.
- **Annual Audit/Review:** Consider an annual audit or financial review by an independent CPA, especially as the church grows or handles significant funds.

## VII. Recommended Tech Stack Options

- **Accounting Software (Cloud-Based Recommended):**
  - **Intuit QuickBooks for Nonprofits:** User-friendly, widely used, good for basic to intermediate needs.
  - **Aplos:** Specifically designed for churches and faith-based nonprofits, strong donor management integration.
  - **Xero:** Modern interface, good integration capabilities, can be adapted for nonprofits.
  - **Sling (if very small budget):** Basic free option, but may lack robust nonprofit features.
- **Donor Management/CRM (if separate):**
  - **ChMS (Church Management System) with integrated giving:** Many offer this functionality (e.g., Planning Center, Church Community Builder).
  - **Bloomerang, Little Green Light:** Dedicated nonprofit CRMs.
- **Document Storage:**
  - **Google Drive/Workspace:** Cost-effective, good for collaboration and storing receipts/invoices.
  - **Microsoft OneDrive/SharePoint:** Similar capabilities.

## VIII. 90-Day Rollout Plan

### Days 1–30: Planning & Setup

- Finalize core requirements and chart of accounts.
- Select and subscribe to chosen accounting software.
- Set up the chart of accounts within the software.
- Define user roles and permissions.
- Establish initial approval workflows.
- Begin data migration planning (historical data, if applicable).

### Days 31–60: Data Migration & Training

- Migrate opening balances and any necessary historical transaction data.
- Train co-pastors, finance committee, and key staff on using the new system.
- Conduct a pilot run of a few weeks of transactions to identify issues.
- Refine workflows and reporting based on pilot feedback.

### Days 61–90: Go-Live & Stabilization

- Officially transition to the new system for all financial operations.
- Perform the first full month-end close and reporting cycle.
- Monitor system performance and user adoption closely.
- Address any remaining issues or user questions.
- Schedule the first finance committee review under the new system.

This plan provides a solid foundation. Flexibility and ongoing review will be key to adapting the system as the church's needs evolve.
