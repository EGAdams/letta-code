## Wrapper Defect Diagnosis

The **FAIL verdict** is a **judge-calibration defect**, not a Mazda failure. The facts:

1. The contract (the dispatch message and the manual's STEP 4 description) explicitly states: *"A `null` category_id is not a failure; she must continue and store uncategorized."*
2. Mazda followed this rule correctly: she called the categorizer, received no category, noted it as a problem in the evidence JSON, and proceeded to store the expense anyway.
3. The expense was successfully stored (`stored: true, expense_id: 1498`), the trace was recorded with accurate evidence, and the dashboard was notified.
4. The **judge** marked this as `FAIL` with `failure_type: wrong_category` and reason `"failed: category_resolved"`.

**The defect:** The judge's rubric does not align with the contract. It treats `category_id=null` as a failure condition when the contract explicitly permits it. This is a **rubric-calibration issue** — the intake rubric (served by `mazda-tools-mcp.service`) must be updated to score `category_id=null` as PASS (or at worst NEEDS-REVIEW) when the expense was still stored.

**Failure taxonomy (per manual §5.2):** Judge-calibration gap — deterministic rules (the rubric) contradict the wrapper's contract.

**Fixable at which layer:** The rubric's scoring logic, not Mazda's wrapper. The judge lives in `rol_finances/tools/self_improving_agent/implementations/finance_mcp/server.py` (or wherever the intake-task rubric is defined).
